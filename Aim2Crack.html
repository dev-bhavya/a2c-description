Allegations and Mixtures

Topic description and key formmulas

Description about the topic

A mixture, as the name suggests is mixing two or more things together.

Simple Mixture

When two different ingredients are mixed together, it is known as a simple mixture.

Compound Mixture

When two or more simple mixtures are mixed together to form another mixture, it is known as a compound mixture. The word ‘Alligation’ literally means ‘linking’. Alligation is the rule which enables us to find the ratio in which two or more elements are mixed together and at what price they are sold to earn profit or face loss. To solve mixture and alligation questions, it must be known that alligation is used to find the mean value of a mixture when the ratio and amount of ingredients mixed are different and also to find the proportion in which the elements are mixed.

Mean Price

The cost of a unit quantity of the mixture is called mean price.

Example:

In the case of the situation where just two groups are being mixed, we can write this as:

Aw = (n1A1 + n2A2)/(n1 + n2)

Rewriting this equation we get:

(n1 + n2)Aw = n1A1 + n2A2

n1(Aw - A1) = n2(A2 - Aw)

Or,

n1/n2 = (A2 - Aw)/(Aw - A1) ➡ (This is alligation equation)
